# Flip 7 Game Rules

## Overview

Flip 7 is a press-your-luck card game where players race to be the first to reach 200 points across multiple rounds.

## Basic Info

- **Players:** 3+ (for 18+ players, use two decks)
- **Age:** 8+
- **Duration:** ~20 minutes
- **Goal:** First player to 200 points wins

## Deck Composition (94 cards)

### Number Cards

The deck contains number cards from 0-12, with quantities matching their values:

- 12 cards with value 12
- 11 cards with value 11
- 10 cards with value 10
- ... continuing down to...
- 1 card with value 1
- 1 card with value 0 (worth no points, but counts toward Flip 7 bonus)

### Action Cards

- **Flip Three:** Target player must accept the next 3 cards
- **Freeze:** Target player banks their points and exits the round
- **Second Chance:** Protects against one duplicate number card

### Score Modifier Cards

- **+2, +4, +6, +8, +10:** Add bonus points to your number card total
- **x2:** Double your number card total (apply before adding other modifiers)

## Setup

1. Shuffle the deck thoroughly
2. Choose a dealer for the round
3. Have pen and paper ready to track scores

## Gameplay

### Starting a Round

1. Dealer deals one card face-up to each player (including themselves)
2. If an Action card appears, pause and resolve it immediately
3. The opening deal ends once each player has been dealt exactly one card, even if that Action card does not remain in front of them

**Important:** After initial dealing, players may have different numbers of cards depending on Action cards dealt, and some players may start their first turn with no cards in front of them.

### Taking Turns

The dealer offers each player in turn order the choice to:

- **Hit:** Receive another card from the deck
- **Stay:** Exit the round and bank current points (must have at least one card)

When hitting, arrange cards as follows:

- Number cards in a single row
- Score Modifier cards placed above the number cards
- Action cards placed above the number cards

### Active vs Inactive Players

- **Active:** Haven't busted or chosen to Stay
- **Inactive:** Have busted or chosen to Stay (flip cards face-down to indicate)

## Action Card Details

### Flip Three

- Target any active player (or yourself if you're the only active player)
- That player must accept the next 3 cards, flipping one at a time
- Stop early if player achieves Flip 7 or busts
- If a Second Chance appears, set it aside for use
- If another Flip Three or Freeze appears, resolve it AFTER all 3 cards are drawn (unless player has busted)

### Freeze

- Target any active player (or yourself if you're the only active player)
- That player immediately banks their current points and exits the round

### Second Chance

- Keep this card in front of you
- If you receive a duplicate number card, discard both the Second Chance and the duplicate
- Only one Second Chance allowed per player at a time
- If dealt a second one, give it to another active player (or discard if none available)
- Discarded at end of round even if unused

## Busting

You **bust** (and score 0 for the round) if you draw a second card with the same number as one already in your line.

**Note:** You cannot bust on Modifier cards or Action cards.

## Flip 7 Bonus

If you successfully get 7 **unique Number cards** face-up in front of you:

- The round ends immediately for all players
- You score an additional **15 bonus points**
- Only Number cards (0-12) count toward this; Action and Modifier cards do not

## End of Round

The round ends when either:

1. All players have busted or chosen to Stay (no active players remain), OR
2. One player achieves Flip 7 (7 unique Number cards)

## Scoring

Calculate your score in this order:

1. Add up all Number card values
2. If you have a x2 multiplier, double that total
3. Add any +2, +4, +6, +8, or +10 bonus points
4. If you achieved Flip 7, add 15 bonus points

**Special Cases:**

- You can score with just Modifier cards and no Number cards (except x2, which needs Number cards to multiply)
- If you busted, you score 0 for that round

### Scoring Example

Number cards: 11 + 5 + 12 = 28 points  
With +4 bonus modifier = 32 points total

## Between Rounds

1. Set all used cards aside (do not shuffle back into deck)
2. Pass remaining deck to the left; that player becomes the new Dealer
3. When the deck runs out, shuffle all discarded cards to form a new deck
4. If reshuffling mid-round, leave all cards in front of players as they are

## Winning the Game

At the end of the round when at least one player reaches 200+ points, the player with the **most points wins**.

## Strategy Tips

- The deck favors higher numbers (more copies of 12s than 1s)
- Zero cards are valuable for the Flip 7 bonus despite being worth no points
- Keep track of which numbers have been played to assess bust risk
- Modifier cards don't count toward Flip 7 but don't cause busts either
